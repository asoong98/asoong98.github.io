Projects

On-Orbit Satellite Life Extension & Augmentation through Autonomous Solar Array Attachment. January 2021 - December 2021

  This student team project is a Northrop Grumman sponsored Multidisciplinary Design Project at the University of Michigan. Over the last few years, Northrop Grumman has proven their ability to send Mission Extension Vehicles (MEVs) into space to dock with existing GEO satellites. After docking, the MEV is able to provide propulsion and attitude control to satellites.
  The student team, Team Orbit, was tasked to take this a step further by researching and implementing a way to augment GEO satellites on-orbit. The team decided to tackle the issue of failed or degrading solar panels by (1) creating a CONOPs of an autonomous on-orbit attachment procedure, (2) prototyping the attachment mechanism, (3) and creating an autonomous vision system capable of localizing a pre-determined attachment location.
  My job on the team is a Software engineer, responsible for creating the autonomous vision system in C++ for localizing a pre-determined attachment point on the satellite yoke. The camera I'm using is the Intel RealSense L515 Lidar camera, capable of simultaneous RGB and Lidar streams. The vision algorithm takes advantage of coordinate transformations and a priori CAD of the satellite we're operating on.
  Given the CAD model of the target satellite, we're able to transfer the 3D information into a point cloud through SolidWorks. The vision algorithm then uses RANSAC and Iterative Closest Points for point cloud registration to find a transformation matrix that best matches the model point cloud of the satellite to the environment point cloud that the camera sees during operation.
  Since we've pre-determined the attachment location in the model coordinate system, we can localize the attachment location with respect to the camera with the transformation matrix.

Basil Leaf Robot. September 2019 - Jnune 2020

  Believe it or not, there has been a labor shortage in the agriculture industry in the Central Valley of California. Farmers and food processing faciliites are stretched thin, placing a strain on the supply chain for food products.
  Stanislaus Food Products is a food processing company specializing in canned tomato goods. One particular section of their processing plant requires a human worker to individually pick and place basil leaves from one conveyor belt to sauce cans on another conveyor belt.
  My Senior Capstone project was to automate this step using a 2D cartesian axis robot with a suction cup gripper and autonomous vision. My role on the team was a Mechatronics Engineer, creating a Python library for stepper motor control in our robot and help develop simple classification algorithms for differentiating rotton and fresh leaves. I also ensured that the mechanical design and software system meshes well with each other.
Due to the COVID-19 Pandemic, we were unable to integrate the vision system with our mechanical design. However, isolated testing showed that the robot was able to quickly reach coordinates repeatably and that the vision algorithms were capable of differentiating rotten and fresh leaves at 73% accuracy.
